FMEA — Types of Risks
Process Risks
Misalignment during die stacking, voids in underfill — like stacking pancakes crooked
Material Risks
CTE mismatch causing warpage or cracking — like mixing clay & metal that expand differently
Electrical Risks
Crosstalk, IR drop, thermomechanical stress on TSVs — like two radios interfering
Mitigation
Redundant interconnects, advanced underfills, Design-for-Reliability (DfR) rules

Phase 3 — Development & Design
Build it right — verify design & bake reliability into every layer
Design Verification — 4 Key Activities
ActivityDefinitionTools
Functional VerificationValidates logic of die-to-die interfaces (UCIe, BoW) & protocol compliance (PCIe/CXL)Synopsys PrimeTime

Design-for-Reliability (DfR) — Reliability Solutions
SolutionDefinitionAnalogy
Interconnect RedundancyExtra TSVs/microbumps in critical paths to survive EM failuresSpare tyre in your car
Stress-Aware LayoutKeep fragile structures away from high-stress zonesDon't put glass near the table edge
Thermal MitigationIntegrate thermal vias, dummy bumps & microchannels for coolingAir vents inside building walls
Underfill & Material SelectionChoose underfills with matched CTE to reduce interfacial stressFlexible grout between tiles
Warpage CompensationAdaptive bonding & dummy structures to balance stressCounterweights on a seesaw
Reliability-Aware Design Flow — 3 Steps
1. Early Risk Assessment
FMEA identifies high-risk zones (microbump fatigue, TSV cracking) on paper before production
2. Co-Design with Packaging
Collaborate with OSATs to optimize substrate & interposer materials together
3. Iterative Simulation Loop
Thermal + mechanical + electrical co-simulation — refine, simulate, repeat until solid

Phase 5 — PHM (Prognostics & Health Management)
After the chip is sold — monitor, adapt & self-heal in the field
3 Core PHM Functions
A. Real-Time Monitoring
Detect early degradation via embedded sensors, on-die telemetry & AI/ML anomaly detection
B. Adaptive Voltage Compensation
Counteract aging effects — DVS, Adaptive Body Biasing & PDN Reconfiguration
C. Self-Healing & Redundancy
Recover from failures without downtime — spare TSVs, laser repair, chiplet failover
A. Real-Time Health Monitoring — Techniques
TechniqueDefinition
Thermal DiodesTrack junction temperature to prevent overheating
Strain SensorsDetect mechanical stress — warpage, underfill cracking
EM MonitorsMeasure resistance drift in power/ground interconnects
ECC LogsTrack soft errors in memory/logic chiplets
BER MonitoringAssess signal degradation in high-speed SerDes links
AI/ML Anomaly DetectionTrained models predict failures by analyzing sensor trends before they happen
B. Adaptive Voltage/Frequency Compensation
TechniqueDefinitionAnalogy
DVS (Dynamic Voltage Scaling)Increase supply voltage Vdd to compensate for aging-induced speed lossEnergy drink when tired at gym
ABB (Adaptive Body Biasing)Adjust transistor body bias to mitigate NBTI/PBTI aging effectsChanging spectacles number as eyesight weakens
PDN ReconfigurationReroute power if EM causes high resistance in certain interconnectsGoogle Maps alternate route during traffic jam
C. Self-Healing & Redundancy Management
TechniqueDefinitionAnalogy
Spare TSVs/MicrobumpsFailed interconnects bypassed using pre-built redundant pathsSpare tyre when puncture occurs
Laser/EEPROM RepairFuses/anti-fuses reconfigure routing — used in HBM stacksElectrician rewiring faulty switch
Chiplet FailoverDisable faulty chiplet & redistribute workloads to healthy onesSubstitute player when cricketer gets injured
PHM Implementation Challenges & Solutions
ChallengeDefinitionSolution
Sensor OverheadSensors consume extra chip area & powerUltra-compact RO-based monitors
Cross-Chiplet Data FusionDifferent chiplets produce data in different formatsIEEE 1687 IJTAG standard interface
False AlarmsNormal variations mistaken for real failuresML-based anomaly filtering

Future Trends in PHM
AI-Driven Predictive Maintenance
ML models forecast failures weeks in advance — like weather forecast for chips
Chiplet-Level PHM Standards
UCIe/BoW interfaces will have health monitoring built-in by default
Self-Testing Chiplets (BIST)
Built-in self-test — chiplets diagnose themselves autonomously like a car's OBD system
Zero-Downtime Systems
Predict + compensate + repair — no unexpected failures in production
